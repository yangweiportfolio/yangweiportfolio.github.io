Building An Instrument

The creation of a wooden guitar.

During the fall semester of 2018 in my Introduction to Mechanical Engineering course, our team, of four, was tasked with the objective of building an instrument that can successfully be tuned and can be used to play a song. Our team decided to build an instrument that was similar to a guitar, The body was designed to be a rectangular prism with two large curve cuts on the surface. The neck of the instrument was designed to have the same length as a standard acoustic guitar and each fret was positioned similar to those found on an acoustic guitar. The position of each fret, on the neck, was researched and measured and was stabilized by cutting slits on the neck and placing the frets within. Four turning keys were placed on the end of the neck and four strings was attached to a key that reached across the whole instrument.

Result: The instrument was able to play tunes similar to an acoustic guitar but at a lower pitch since there is no opening in the middle of the body but instead there were openings on the sides. Also, less pitches can be played since there as only four tuning keys compared to the six that an acoustic guitar has.
